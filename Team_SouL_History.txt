Comprehensive Historical Analysis of Team SouL (2018–2026): Roster Evolution, Competitive Performance, and Ecosystem Impact in PUBG Mobile and BGMI
1. Overview
Team SouL stands as a foundational institution within the South Asian mobile esports ecosystem. Originating in India, the organization has exerted profound influence over the competitive and commercial trajectories of PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds Mobile (PUBG Mobile) and its regional successor, Battlegrounds Mobile India (BGMI).1 Formally established on December 21, 2018, the team was founded by Naman "MortaL" Mathur alongside Animesh "Thug" Agarwal and Lokesh "Goldy" Jain.1 Operating currently under the parent corporate entity S8UL Esports—a conglomerate formed via the strategic merger of Team SouL and 8bit Creatives—the organization operates dual divisions of high-tier competitive esports and mass-market gaming content creation.4
The timeline scope of this analysis encompasses the organization's inception in late 2018 through the culmination of the first quarter of 2026. This period is critical for understanding the maturation of mobile esports in India, as it covers the original PUBG Mobile boom (2018–2019), the systemic disruption caused by the Indian government's ban on the application (2020), the transitional revival marked by the launch of BGMI (2021), and the highly structured, high-capital modern competitive era (2022–2026).5
Data indicates that Team SouL is among the most commercially successful and widely viewed mobile esports franchises globally. The organization has amassed approximately $718,647 in cumulative competitive winnings across its history, with $509,165 of that total derived specifically from BGMI tournaments since the game's introduction.1 To date, the BGMI division has participated in 83 verified tournaments, playing 677 official matches with a format distribution of 70% online and 30% offline play.1
Beyond competitive prize pools, Team SouL's strategic value is deeply tied to its viewership metrics, which regularly eclipse those of traditional sporting events in the region. Verified viewership records demonstrate massive audience retention across multiple titles and years. The team's all-time peak viewership record stands at 577,685 concurrent viewers, achieved during the Battlegrounds Mobile India Series 2026.1 Historical peaks include 525,131 viewers during the PUBG Mobile Club Open Fall Split Global Finals (2019), 485,132 viewers during the Battlegrounds Mobile India Series 2025, and 421,769 viewers at the S-Tier PUBG Mobile World Invitational 2022.1 Supported by premier sponsors such as IQOO, the organization operates a highly sophisticated ecosystem where competitive success directly feeds content engagement, mitigating financial risks associated with the inherent volatility of esports tournament outcomes.1
This document provides a strictly analytical, fact-checked, and source-verified historical record of Team SouL, explicitly tracking every confirmed roster iteration, tournament placement, and strategic pivot from 2018 to 2026.

Major Highlights
	•	Failures: The heralded return of Sc0utOP alongside elite IGL Mavi was expected to dominate the domestic circuit. Instead, the lineup suffered a catastrophic failure of synergy. Across over twenty verified C-Tier and B-Tier tournaments spanning August to December 2021, the team routinely finished in the bottom quartile of lobbies (e.g., 20th at BGMI EWar, 18th at Chemin Esports, 23rd at Red Bull M.E.O.).17 Sc0utOP did secure a "Best Clutch (Fan Vote)" award worth $680 at Skyesports Championship 3.0, but this was a rare individual highlight during a period of acute macro-level dysfunction.2
	•	Turning Points: The dismal 16th-place finish at the crucial BGIS 2021: The Grind qualifier proved that the existing roster structure—which relied heavily on legacy talent from the 2019 era—was fundamentally incompatible with the evolved, mechanically demanding BGMI meta.17 This statistical collapse forced management to authorize a complete, high-capital roster overhaul executed in January 2022. Mavi and Sc0utOP departed the organization for TeamXSpark in mid-October following the failure of the initial experiment.2
Year: 2022
The 2022 season represented a critical strategic reset for S8UL management. By abandoning legacy roster constructs in favor of aggressive, highly scouted mechanical talent guided by a dedicated coaching staff, Team SouL entered its "Superteam" era, culminating in historic financial and competitive success.

Major Highlights
	•	Wins: The acquisition of Omega as IGL fundamentally altered the team's trajectory. Termed "The Humble IGL," his macro-level shot-calling enabled the raw mechanical prowess of newly acquired entry fragger Goblin.18 This synergy peaked on June 12 at the A-Tier Battlegrounds Mobile India Pro Series (BMPS) Season 1, where the team decisively captured 1st place and $95,935.17 Goblin's individual performance during this tournament was historically dominant; he swept the award categories, securing MVP ($3,198), Rampage Freak ($1,279), Fan Favorite Player ($1,279), and three separate Player of the Day honors.19
	•	Turning Points: Validating their domestic supremacy, the squad represented the region at the S-Tier PUBG Mobile World Invitational 2022. While finishing 11th overall ($58,500) amidst intense international competition, Hector demonstrated profound mechanical capability, winning the "Eagle Eye" award and its accompanying $5,000 prize.17 At the BGMI Masters Series in July, Hector further amassed utility-based honors, winning Week 1 Saviour, Week 2 Doctor, and Finals Saviour ($314 each).20

While the team secured numerous B-Tier and C-Tier victories, the year culminated in the official retirement of its legacy founders from active play and the mass departure of the Superteam core.

Major Highlights
	•	Failures & Stagnation: While the team successfully farmed B-Tier and C-Tier events—securing 1st place at Fight Night Season 5, OS Invitational Season 1, and Battle For Revolution Season 2 (where Goblin secured MVP and Top Fragger awards worth $962 combined)—their performance in definitive A-Tier competitions regressed significantly.17 A disastrous 17th-place collapse at BGMS Season 2 and a middling 5th-place finish at BMPS 2023 highlighted a stagnation in tactical execution. Opposing teams had analyzed and countered Omega's rotational strategies, and the roster could no longer win purely on mechanical dominance.17
	•	Turning Points: On January 16, founding members MortaL and VipeR officially retired from all active competitive play to focus exclusively on content and organizational management.2 Following the tactical stagnation in the latter half of the year, the core roster—Omega, AkshaT, Hector, and Neyo—departed the organization simultaneously on December 23, 2023, eventually transferring to Carnival Gaming. Team SouL released a formal statement acknowledging their "immeasurable" impact, closing the chapter on the Superteam era.2
Year: 2024
Faced with a complete vacuum in their competitive roster, Team SouL executed a high-profile, turnkey acquisition of the ex-Blind Esports core on January 1, 2024. This strategy bypassed the arduous process of organic roster building, instantly restoring the organization to championship status.

Major Highlights
	•	Wins: The acquisition of Manya and his squad generated immediate returns. The team demonstrated elite consistency early in the year, securing 2nd at the A-Tier ESL Snapdragon Pro Series 2024 ($14,425) and placing 4th at the highly competitive BGIS 2024 ($17,993).17 The pinnacle of the season occurred on August 11, when the Manya-led roster dominated the A-Tier BGMI Masters Series Season 3, capturing 1st place and $65,515.17

Year: 2025

Despite substantial mid-year turbulence, the team restructured effectively to secure several high-value A-Tier and B-Tier victories in Q4.

Major Highlights
	•	Wins: The victory at the A-Tier Battlegrounds Mobile India Series (BGIS) 2026 definitively validated the current roster iteration. Occurring on March 29, the $105,505 prize stands as the organization's largest single documented payout.17 Individual accolades reinforced this systemic dominance: NakuL was awarded Best IGL, proving his transition out of Manya's shadow, while newly acquired talent LEGIT was named Finals MVP.2
	•	Turning Points: The commercial impact of this victory was equally historic. The BGIS 2026 event set an all-time peak viewership record for Team SouL at 577,685 concurrent viewers, inextricably linking their competitive success with the S8UL brand's overarching audience capture strategy.1 This dual commercial and competitive peak defines the modern state of the organization.
3. Key Eras
The chronological and statistical data organically segregates Team SouL's history into four distinct strategic eras, characterized by their roster architecture, competitive output, and organizational response to external market conditions.
1. The OG Era (2018–2019)
Built around the foundational quintet of MortaL, Owais, RonaK, VipeR, and Slayer, this period defined the infancy of Indian mobile esports.2 Operating in the pre-ban PUBG Mobile ecosystem, this era was marked by early statistical dominance based largely on individual mechanical disparity rather than advanced macro-rotational strategies. The squad yielded 1st-place victories at PMIS 2019 and PMCO Spring India, capturing over $100,000.7 The era concluded abruptly on September 10, 2019, when internal friction led to the fractured departure of Owais, RonaK, and Sc0utOP.2
2. The Transition & Ban Era (2020–2021)
A strategically dormant and highly volatile period driven directly by the geopolitical PUBG Mobile app ban in India. The team was forced to rely heavily on rotating, temporary cast members (Sangwan, Blaezi, AMAN, Mavi) to tread water.2 Statistically, this was the organization's weakest competitive phase, evidenced by late 2021 results plummeting to 16th–23rd place finishes in standard B and C-Tier lobbies (e.g., Red Bull M.E.O., BGMI EWar).17 However, the primary success of this era was corporate rather than competitive: the 2020 strategic merger forming S8UL Esports, which successfully secured the brand's long-term financial viability through diversified content creation.4
3. The Superteam Era (2022–2023)
Triggered by the January 13, 2022, acquisition of Omega and AkshaT, followed subsequently by Goblin and Hector, this era redefined Team SouL's tactical approach.2 The organization shifted from fielding legacy influencer-players to employing dedicated mechanical specialists operating under strict coaching frameworks. The apex of this period was the June 2022 BMPS Season 1 victory ($95,935) and PMWI 2022 global representation.17 However, severe tactical stagnation in late 2023 against an evolving domestic meta led to the complete, synchronized dissolution of this core on December 23, 2023.2
4. The Rebuild & Modern Era (2024–2026)
Following the December 2023 exodus, S8UL management utilized significant capital to acquire the highly cohesive former Blind Esports roster (Manya, NakuL, Jokerr, Rony, Spower) on January 1, 2024.2 This turnkey approach immediately restored A-Tier dominance with a BGMS Season 3 victory.17 Further iterations across 2025—which involved shedding Manya, reintegrating the historic fragger Goblin, and scouting raw talents like LEGIT and Thunder—forged the current optimal meta-roster. This culminated in the historic BGIS 2026 championship victory, generating record prize money ($105,505) and peak viewership (577,685).1
